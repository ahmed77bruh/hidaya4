Masʾalah 3 (cont.) — Ḥanafī Rebuttal

1.إِذَا غُمِّيَ الرَّهْنُ فَهُوَ بِمَا فِيهِ — if the marhūn's value becomes unclear after halāk, it's valued by estimation.
2. Ijmāʿ of Ṣaḥābah & Tābiʿīn: marhūn is maḍmūn (though they differ on kayfiyyah) — al-Marghīnānī: "the amānah view breaks this ijmāʿ" (khariq lahu).
3.لَا يَغْلَقُ الرَّهْنُ reread: = no automatic total forfeiture/ownership-transfer to the murtahin — not "no ḍamān" (per al-Karkhī, from the Salaf).
al-Kifāyah — kayfiyyat al-ḍamān (in-depth)

Point 1's "ishtabahat" (if the value becomes uncertain) is glossed: if the rāhin says "I don't know how much it was worth" and the murtahin says the same, it's valued as estimated (this taʾwīl is narrated from Abū Ja'far).

The Ṣaḥābah's kayfiyyah dispute (why al-Marghīnānī says "mukhtalifūn fī kayfiyyatihi" but still ijmāʿ on the core): Abū Bakr & ʿAlī — maḍmūn bi-qīmatihi (by its full value); ʿUmar & Ibn Masʿūd — maḍmūn bi'l-aqall min qīmatihi wa min al-dayn (by whichever is less, its value or the debt); Ibn ʿAbbās — maḍmūn bi'l-dayn (by the debt amount itself). All agree ḍamān exists — they only differ on the measure. [al-Kifāyah 70/9]

"lā yaghlaq al-rahn" — full gloss: no one from the language/fiqh scholars understood this as negating ḍamān from the murtahin. al-Karkhī reports from the Salaf (including Ṭāwūs, Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī) that they agreed the meaning is: the marhūn does not become owned by the murtahin merely by the dayn's term expiring — refuting the jāhilī practice (via al-Zuhrī) where people stipulated "if I don't pay by such a date, the rahn is yours." The Prophet ﷺ nullified that stipulation with "lā yaghlaq al-rahn." A minority (Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab) read it as "the rāhin isn't bound by a term-based sale unless he actually sells at that time" — same conclusion, different route.

"lahu ghunmuhu wa ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" reread by the Ḥanafīs: ghunm = zawāʾid (increase/offspring of the marhūn) belongs to the rāhin (like a muḍārib analogy — the "owner of capital" gets the growth); ghurm = the rāhin bears the marhūn's own decrease in value, and if halāk occurs, "the meaning of ghurm" is specifically: nafaqat al-rahn ḥāl qiyāmihi, wa'l-kafan ḥāl mawtihi (its upkeep while alive, its shroud if it dies) — i.e. these two specific costs fall on the rāhin, not that the whole dayn survives halāk. [al-Kifāyah 71/9]

Dalīl — Yad al-Istīfāʾ & Ḥikmah

1. Thābit li'l-murtahin: yad al-istīfāʾ = milk al-yad wa'l-ḥabs (possessory right aimed at eventual recovery) — because rahn implies continuous detention (ḥabs dāʾim).
2. Qurʾān parallel — ﴿كُلُّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ رَهِينَةٌ﴾ [74:38]; poetic shāhid (bayt of Zuhayr): وَفَارَقْتُكَ بِرَهْنٍ لَا فِكَاكَ لَهُ … يَوْمَ الْوَدَاعِ فَأَمْسَى الرَّهْنُ قَدْ غَلِقَا
3. Ḥikmah: rahn = wathīqah for istīfāʾ → gives amn min al-juḥūd (security against denial) + makes rāhin ʿājiz ʿan al-intifāʿ → rāhin hastens to pay the dayn.

Core Ḥanafī Logic — the Ribā Point

StepPoint
1Istīfāʾ thābit min wajh, taqarrara bi'l-halāk — halāk of the marhūn is a (partial) satisfaction of the dayn, now confirmed/settled.
2Law istawfāhu thāniyan → yuʾaddī ilā'l-ribā — if the murtahin then collected the full dayn too, that's a double recovery = ribā.
3Bi-khilāf ḥālat al-qiyām — while the marhūn is intact, this "notional" istīfāʾ is undone by returning it, so no double-dip issue there.
4Istīfāʾ occurs via al-māliyyah (the value); the ʿayn itself remains amānah — hence nafaqah/kafan of the marhūn fall on the rāhin, not the murtahin.
Also: qabḍ al-rahn does NOT substitute for qabḍ al-sharāʾ if the murtahin later buys the marhūn — amānah-qabḍ can't stand in for ḍamān-qabḍ.

Rebutting Shāfiʿī's Ṣiyānah Argument

1. Muqtaḍā al-ʿaqd = thubūt yad al-istīfāʾ — this is what realizes ṣiyānah (not despite halāk-istīfāʾ, but because of it).
2. Barāʾat al-dhimmah (discharge) is min ḍarūrātihi (a necessary side-effect), not the primary maqṣūd — same structure as ḥawālah.
Uṣūl summary + branch masʾalah — in-depth

al-Ḥāṣil (the upshot): for us [Ḥanafīs], ḥukm al-rahn = the marhūn becomes muḥtabas (held) against its dayn by establishing yad al-istīfāʾ over it. For him [al-Shāfiʿī], the dayn attaches to the ʿayn such that istīfāʾ only ever happens via an actual sale (bayʿ) — no automatic value-transfer on halāk.

Al-Marghīnānī notes several masāʾil branch from these two uṣūl, which he says he enumerated in a work called Kifāyat al-Muntahā — a compilation of Ḥanafī–Shāfiʿī khilāfiyyāt on rahn (distinct from al-Kākī's al-Kifāyah fī Sharḥ al-Hidāyah). One example given: the rāhin is barred from reclaiming the marhūn temporarily for his own use [Ḥanafī] — since that defeats the required continuous detention — whereas per al-Shāfiʿī he's not barred, since occasional use doesn't conflict with the marhūn's designation for eventual sale.

New Matn — Dayn Maḍmūn Required

1.قَالَ: «وَلَا يَصِحُّ الرَّهْنُ إِلَّا بِدَيْنٍ مَضْمُونٍ» — rahn is valid only against a dayn maḍmūn. Sharḥ: ḥukmuhu = thubūt yad al-istīfāʾ, and istīfāʾ presupposes an established wujūb.
Objection + response — in-depth

Objection: this wording seems to exclude rahn taken against aʿyān maḍmūnah bi-anfusihā (e.g. a ghaṣb-claim on a specific object) — such rahn is valid even though there's technically no "dayn" yet.

Response (per the majority of the mashāyikh): the wājib aṣlī in such cases is actually the qīmah (value) — returning the ʿayn itself is merely a mukhalliṣ (a way out/discharge), not the obligation itself — and that qīmah-obligation is a dayn. Proof: kafālah is valid for such claims too, and kafālah requires a dayn-type obligation. Even though the qīmah-obligation only becomes "due" after halāk, it becomes obligatory at the moment of halāk by virtue of the prior qabḍ — which is why its value is assessed as of [the day of that qabḍ — cuts off, continues page 3].

Takhrīj — richer than al-Dirāyah alone

1001 "lā yaghlaq al-rahn... ghunmuhu... ghurmuhu" — al-Dirāyah showed mostly mursal chains + al-Dāraquṭnī's ḥasan muttaṣil. This Vol-7 PDF adds: Ibn Ḥibbān included it in his Ṣaḥīḥ; al-Ḥākim (al-Mustadrak, Kitāb al-Buyūʿ) graded it ṣaḥīḥ ʿalā sharṭ al-Shaykhayn though neither Bukhārī nor Muslim actually collected it.
Tanbīh on "qālahā thalāthan": al-Dirāyah (Ibn Ḥajar) said "lam ajidhu" for this specific detail. Worth flagging as an open question rather than fully resolved either way.
1002 "dhahaba ḥaqquka" — al-Dirāyah graded it mursal (Abū Dāwūd's Marāsīl + Ibn Abī Shaybah). This PDF adds al-Ṭaḥāwī's own defense (Sharḥ Maʿānī al-Āthār): when the objection "hādhā munqaṭiʿ" (this chain is broken) is raised against the Ḥanafīs, al-Ṭaḥāwī replies that al-Shāfiʿī's own "lā yaghlaq" report is equally munqaṭiʿ/mursal — "in kāna al-munqaṭiʿ ḥujjatan laka ʿalaynā, fa'l-munqaṭiʿ ḥujjatun lanā ʿalayka" ("if a broken-chain report is a proof for you against us, a broken-chain report is [equally] a proof for us against you").
