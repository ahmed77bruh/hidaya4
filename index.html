Taʿrīf

1.الرَّهْنُ لُغَةً: حَبْسُ الشَّيْءِ — lughatan: detaining a thing.
2.شَرْعًا: جَعْلُ الشَّيْءِ مَحْبُوسًا بِحَقٍّ يُمْكِنُ اسْتِيفَاؤُهُ مِنْهُ كَالدَّيْنِ — sharʿan: a thing held as security for a ḥaqq (usually dayn), istīfāʾ obtainable from it.
al-Bināyah — in-depth

Lughah: al-ʿAynī notes the same root appears outside contracts: ﴿كُلُّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ رَهِينَةٌ﴾ [المدثر: ٣٨] — "every soul is rahīnah (held) for what it earned," i.e. detained by the consequence of its own misdeeds. Also: māʾun rāhin = still water, niʿmatun rāhinah = a lasting blessing — the root's core sense is fixity/permanence (al-thabāt wa'l-dawām). "Rahn" (the maṣdar) is also used to mean the marhūn itself; plural rahn/rihān.

Sharʿan — why "ḥaqq" not "dayn": deliberate, because rahn is valid not only for a dayn but also for a claim from ghaṣb (usurpation); ḥaqq covers both. "Kal-duyūn" is a deliberate exclusion (iḥtirāz) of pledging khamr (wine) and of taking a rahn to secure ḥudūd or qiṣāṣ — those don't admit istīfāʾ through a substitute value.

al-Qudduri's alternate taʿrīf: rahn shar'an is an ʿaqd wathīqah (contract of security) — distinguishing it from kafālah/ḥawālah (wathīqah bi-dhimmah, security attached to a person's liability) and from a sale-item still in the seller's hand.

Six-part breakdown al-ʿAynī gives: maʿnā lughatan · maʿnā sharʿan · sabab (the creditor's muṭālabah/demand for security) · rukn (ījāb alone per the Ḥanafīs; some require qabūl too) · ḥukm (milk ḥabs al-marhūn wa-ḥaqq al-muṭālabah bi'l-bayʿ) · ḥikmah (ḥuṣūl al-naẓar min al-jānibayn — mutual benefit to both sides).

Dalāʾil (mukhtaṣar)

1. Qurʾān — ﴿فَرِهَانٌ مَقْبُوضَةٌ﴾ [2:283]
2. Ḥadīth — النبي ﷺ اشترى طعامًا من يهودي ورهنه دِرْعَه (muttafaq ʿalayh)
3. Ijmāʿ — munʿaqid (undisputed)
4. Qiyās — wathīqah, like kafālah
Dalāʾil — in-depth

Qurʾān: the āyah was revealed about a debt made while traveling (safar) without a scribe available — Allāh permits substituting a rahn maqbūḍah for writing/witnessing. "Maqbūḍah" is itself the seed of the qabḍ discussion in Masʾalah 1.

Ḥadīth: the well-known report that the Prophet ﷺ bought food on credit from a Jewish merchant in Madīnah and left his armor as security — establishes both legitimacy and precedent (fiʿl) at once.

Ijmāʿ: beyond naql, there is no dispute among the fuqahāʾ that rahn as an institution is legitimate — the differences that follow (this whole kitāb) are about its details, not its basic mashrūʿiyyah.

Qiyās: rahn and kafālah are both wathāʾiq — kafālah secures fixing (wujūb) a debt onto a second person, rahn secures recovery (istīfāʾ) of a debt from a thing. Since kafālah is agreed legitimate, so is rahn.

Masʾalah 1 — Inʿiqād vs. Tamām

Matnالرَّهْنُ يَنْعَقِدُ بِالْإِيجَابِ وَالْقَبُولِ وَيَتِمُّ بِالْقَبْضِ
RuknḤanafī ījāb alone (ʿaqd tabarruʿ, like hibah); qabḍ = sharṭ al-luzūmMālik yalzam bi-nafs al-ʿaqd (māl both sides → like bayʿ)
Qabḍ (manqūl)ẓāhir riwāyah: takhliyah kāfī (asaḥḥ)Abū Yūsuf naql required (ḍamān yabtadiʾ, mithl ghaṣb)
Masʾalah 1 — in-depth

Ḥanafī rukn: rukn is ījāb by itself, since rahn is ʿaqd tabarruʿ — it is completed by the mutabarriʿ alone (like hibah/ṣadaqah); qabḍ is only sharṭ al-luzūm, not a rukn. Rukn = without which the ʿaqd doesn't exist; sharṭ al-luzūm = needed only for bindingness.

Mālik's view & why: rahn is lāzim the instant ījāb+qabūl occur — (a) unlike a pure gift it has māl on both sides (marhūn vs. dayn), closer to bayʿ; (b) it's a wathīqah like kafālah, which binds on ʿaqd alone.

Ḥanafī rebuttal: (1) grammar — "فَرِهَانٌ مَّقْبُوضَةٌ": a maṣdar-phrase right after fāʾ in a conditional's jawāb functions as a command in Arabic usage — Allāh in effect commands "let there be a pledge, taken into possession," placing qabḍ at the heart of what makes rahn function. (2) rahn is tabarruʿ — the rāhin gets nothing owed in return and cannot be judicially compelled (yujbaru), so it needs a concrete imḍāʾ (execution) to finalize, as in waṣiyyah — that imḍāʾ is qabḍ.

Takhliyah vs. naql: ẓāhir al-riwāyah — mere takhliyah (clearing access, e.g. handing over keys) suffices, by analogy to qabḍ of the mabīʿ in a sale. Abū Yūsuf differs for manqūlāt specifically: this qabḍ originates ḍamān from scratch (ibtidāʾan), like ghaṣb — unlike a buyer's qabḍ, which merely shifts existing ḍamān from seller to buyer — so he requires actual physical naql. Al-Marghīnānī: the first view (takhliyah) is asaḥḥ.

Masʾalah 2 — Sharṭ al-Qabḍ & Khiyār

1.قَبَضَهُ مَحُوزًا مُفَرَّغًا مُتَمَيِّزًا → تَمَّ الْعَقْدُ → lazima
2. Before qabḍ → rāhin bi'l-khiyār: yusallim aw yarjiʿ ʿan al-rahn (maqṣūd not yet ḥāṣil)
Masʾalah 2 — in-depth

The marhūn must be maḥūz (fully gathered into the murtahin's hold), mufarragh (cleared of any competing use/mixture), and mutamayyiz (clearly identifiable as this specific object, not a vague undivided share) — only then is qabḍ "complete" and the ʿaqd reaches tamām/luzūm.

Before that: the rāhin keeps full khiyār — he isn't yet bound and may simply retract, because the maqṣūd of rahn (securing the murtahin's ability to obtain istīfāʾ) only functions once he actually holds the marhūn — so luzūm cannot attach any earlier than qabḍ.

Masʾalah 3 — Ḍamān al-Marhūn

ḤanafīShāfiʿī
Statusmaḍmūn — دخل في ضمانهamānah
If halākdayn reduced by qīmah of marhūndayn stays in full — rāhin loses marhūn for nothing
Dalīlﷺ to murtahin (horse died): «ذَهَبَ حَقُّكَ»«لَا يَغْلَقُ الرَّهْنُ… لِصَاحِبِهِ غُنْمُهُ وَعَلَيْهِ غُرْمُهُ» + qiyās ʿalā halāk al-ṣakk
Masʾalah 3 — in-depth

Shāfiʿī's case: the marhūn is pure amānah — the murtahin bears no ḍamān. If it's destroyed without his fault, the rāhin still owes the full dayn. Dalīl: "lā yaghlaq al-rahn... lahu ghunmuhu wa-ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" — any ghunm (growth) of the marhūn belongs to the rāhin, so any ghurm (loss) is his too. Qiyās: rahn is like a debt-document (ṣakk) — losing the paper doesn't erase the debt; also, the whole point of a rahn is to increase ṣiyānah (security) for the creditor — if the debt could vanish whenever the marhūn is destroyed, that defeats the purpose of taking security at all.

Marghīnānī's gloss on the same ḥadīth: "lā yaghlaq" means the marhūn does not automatically become the murtahin's property in full settlement merely upon the rāhin's default (no automatic foreclosure) — not that it carries zero ḍamān. Same naṣṣ, opposite reading.

Ḥanafī rebuttal (opens here, continues page 2): ﷺ told a murtahin whose pledged horse died, "dhahaba ḥaqquka" — your right (to that extent) is gone — directly supporting that halāk of the marhūn does extinguish part of the dayn, because the murtahin is ḍāmin, not a mere amānah-holder.

Takhrīj — al-Dirāyah (quick grading)

1000 (buying food, pledged armor) — muttafaq ʿalayh, ʿĀʾishah. Wordings vary: ilā ajal / dirʿ min ḥadīd / thalāthīn ṣāʿ.

1002 (dhahaba ḥaqquka) — mursal (Abū Dāwūd al-Marāsīl, via ʿAṭāʾ; also Ibn Abī Shaybah).
al-Dirāyah — full isnād notes

1001 — chains in detail: (a) Ibn Ḥibbān, via Sufyān ← Ziyād ibn Saʿd ← al-Zuhrī ← Saʿīd ← Abū Hurayrah; ṣaḥḥaḥahu al-Ḥākim. (b) al-Dāraquṭnī, muttaṣil route, graded ḥasan muttaṣil; also authenticated by ʿAbd al-Ḥaqq, accepted by Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr. (c) ʿAbd al-Razzāq ← Maʿmar ← al-Zuhrī ← Ibn al-Musayyab — mursal; includes al-Zuhrī confirming this addresses the pre-Islamic saying "if I don't bring your money, the rahn is yours," with the addition "if it perishes, this one's right does not disappear — only the value perishes from the rahn's owner; lahu ghunmuhu wa-ʿalayhi ghurmuhu." (d) Same mursal chain also via al-Thawrī, Ibn Abī Shaybah/Wakīʿ, and al-Shāfiʿī/Ibn Abī Fudayk — all ← Ibn Abī Dhiʾb ← al-Zuhrī. Al-Shāfiʿī's own gloss: ghunmuhu = increase, ghurmuhu = decrease and halāk. (e) Abū Dāwūd (al-Marāsīl): notes "lahu ghunmuhu wa-ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" may be Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab's own words (mudraj), not the Prophet's ﷺ. Parallel report from Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī describes the jāhilī practice the ḥadīth corrects.

Tanbīh: Ibn Ḥajar explicitly flags "qālahā thalāthan" (said it three times) as "lam ajidhu" — not found in any source he traced. Worth knowing before citing that detail as established.

1000: Ibn Ḥajar cross-refers that something on this ḥadīth already preceded at the start of Kitāb al-Buyūʿ.

1002: via ʿAṭāʾ — a man pledged a horse, it died in the murtahin's hand, Prophet ﷺ said "dhahaba ḥaqquka" — mursal in both Abū Dāwūd's Marāsīl and Ibn Abī Shaybah. The entire Ḥanafī rebuttal on this page rests on a mursal report — al-Marghīnānī will need to argue for its acceptability, or argue the ruling by other means, on the next page.

Ṣūrat al-Masʾalah

ʿAmr & Bakr

ʿAmr owes Bakr $1,000 dayn. Pledges a laptop (qīmah $800) — ījāb; Bakr qabūl.

Before qabḍ: ʿAmr may still rajaʿa (khiyār) — Bakr can't force delivery.

After qabḍ, halāk (no fault of Bakr):

  • Ḥanafī: $800 of dayn extinguished → ʿAmr owes $200.
  • Shāfiʿī: ʿAmr still owes the full $1,000.
Walkthrough — in-depth

Scenario A (before qabḍ): ʿAmr offers the laptop (ījāb), Bakr accepts (qabūl) — the ʿaqd is munʿaqid but not yet lāzim (it's tabarruʿ, like hibah). Before ʿAmr actually hands it over, he changes his mind — since qabḍ hasn't happened, this is within his khiyār and Bakr cannot compel delivery. Had ʿAmr delivered it and Bakr received it maḥūzan mufarraghan mutamayyizan, the ʿaqd becomes lāzim and ʿAmr can no longer withdraw.

Scenario B (after qabḍ, halāk occurs): ʿAmr delivers, Bakr takes proper qabḍ — the laptop is now marhūn in Bakr's ḍamān. A week later, with no fault of Bakr's, it's destroyed (say, stolen beyond recovery).

Per al-Shāfiʿī: the laptop was amānah in Bakr's hand — Bakr bears no risk, so none of ʿAmr's dayn is reduced; ʿAmr still owes the full $1,000 and has lost his $800 laptop for nothing.

Per the Ḥanafī view (the position al-Marghīnānī is building toward, via "dhahaba ḥaqquka"): the laptop was maḍmūn in Bakr's possession, not mere amānah — its halāk operates as an implicit istīfāʾ up to its qīmah. $800 of the dayn is treated as satisfied, so ʿAmr now owes Bakr only the remaining $200.

Masʾalah 3 (cont.) — Ḥanafī Rebuttal

1.إِذَا غُمِّيَ الرَّهْنُ فَهُوَ بِمَا فِيهِ — if the marhūn's value becomes unclear after halāk, it's valued by estimation.
2. Ijmāʿ of Ṣaḥābah & Tābiʿīn: marhūn is maḍmūn (though they differ on kayfiyyah) — al-Marghīnānī: "the amānah view breaks this ijmāʿ" (khariq lahu).
3.لَا يَغْلَقُ الرَّهْنُ reread: = no automatic total forfeiture/ownership-transfer to the murtahin — not "no ḍamān" (per al-Karkhī, from the Salaf).
al-Kifāyah — kayfiyyat al-ḍamān (in-depth)

Point 1's "ishtabahat" (if the value becomes uncertain) is glossed: if the rāhin says "I don't know how much it was worth" and the murtahin says the same, it's valued as estimated (this taʾwīl is narrated from Abū Ja'far).

The Ṣaḥābah's kayfiyyah dispute (why al-Marghīnānī says "mukhtalifūn fī kayfiyyatihi" but still ijmāʿ on the core): Abū Bakr & ʿAlī — maḍmūn bi-qīmatihi (by its full value); ʿUmar & Ibn Masʿūd — maḍmūn bi'l-aqall min qīmatihi wa min al-dayn (by whichever is less, its value or the debt); Ibn ʿAbbās — maḍmūn bi'l-dayn (by the debt amount itself). All agree ḍamān exists — they only differ on the measure. [al-Kifāyah 70/9]

"lā yaghlaq al-rahn" — full gloss: no one from the language/fiqh scholars understood this as negating ḍamān from the murtahin. al-Karkhī reports from the Salaf (including Ṭāwūs, Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī) that they agreed the meaning is: the marhūn does not become owned by the murtahin merely by the dayn's term expiring — refuting the jāhilī practice (via al-Zuhrī) where people stipulated "if I don't pay by such a date, the rahn is yours." The Prophet ﷺ nullified that stipulation with "lā yaghlaq al-rahn." A minority (Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab) read it as "the rāhin isn't bound by a term-based sale unless he actually sells at that time" — same conclusion, different route.

"lahu ghunmuhu wa ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" reread by the Ḥanafīs: ghunm = zawāʾid (increase/offspring of the marhūn) belongs to the rāhin (like a muḍārib analogy — the "owner of capital" gets the growth); ghurm = the rāhin bears the marhūn's own decrease in value, and if halāk occurs, "the meaning of ghurm" is specifically: nafaqat al-rahn ḥāl qiyāmihi, wa'l-kafan ḥāl mawtihi (its upkeep while alive, its shroud if it dies) — i.e. these two specific costs fall on the rāhin, not that the whole dayn survives halāk. [al-Kifāyah 71/9]

Dalīl — Yad al-Istīfāʾ & Ḥikmah

1. Thābit li'l-murtahin: yad al-istīfāʾ = milk al-yad wa'l-ḥabs (possessory right aimed at eventual recovery) — because rahn implies continuous detention (ḥabs dāʾim).
2. Qurʾān parallel — ﴿كُلُّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ رَهِينَةٌ﴾ [74:38]; poetic shāhid (bayt of Zuhayr): وَفَارَقْتُكَ بِرَهْنٍ لَا فِكَاكَ لَهُ … يَوْمَ الْوَدَاعِ فَأَمْسَى الرَّهْنُ قَدْ غَلِقَا
3. Ḥikmah: rahn = wathīqah for istīfāʾ → gives amn min al-juḥūd (security against denial) + makes rāhin ʿājiz ʿan al-intifāʿ → rāhin hastens to pay the dayn.

Core Ḥanafī Logic — the Ribā Point

StepPoint
1Istīfāʾ thābit min wajh, taqarrara bi'l-halāk — halāk of the marhūn is a (partial) satisfaction of the dayn, now confirmed/settled.
2Law istawfāhu thāniyan → yuʾaddī ilā'l-ribā — if the murtahin then collected the full dayn too, that's a double recovery = ribā.
3Bi-khilāf ḥālat al-qiyām — while the marhūn is intact, this "notional" istīfāʾ is undone by returning it, so no double-dip issue there.
4Istīfāʾ occurs via al-māliyyah (the value); the ʿayn itself remains amānah — hence nafaqah/kafan of the marhūn fall on the rāhin, not the murtahin.
Also: qabḍ al-rahn does NOT substitute for qabḍ al-sharāʾ if the murtahin later buys the marhūn — amānah-qabḍ can't stand in for ḍamān-qabḍ.

Rebutting Shāfiʿī's Ṣiyānah Argument

1. Muqtaḍā al-ʿaqd = thubūt yad al-istīfāʾ — this is what realizes ṣiyānah (not despite halāk-istīfāʾ, but because of it).
2. Barāʾat al-dhimmah (discharge) is min ḍarūrātihi (a necessary side-effect), not the primary maqṣūd — same structure as ḥawālah.
Uṣūl summary + branch masʾalah — in-depth

al-Ḥāṣil (the upshot): for us [Ḥanafīs], ḥukm al-rahn = the marhūn becomes muḥtabas (held) against its dayn by establishing yad al-istīfāʾ over it. For him [al-Shāfiʿī], the dayn attaches to the ʿayn such that istīfāʾ only ever happens via an actual sale (bayʿ) — no automatic value-transfer on halāk.

Al-Marghīnānī notes several masāʾil branch from these two uṣūl, which he says he enumerated in a work called Kifāyat al-Muntahā — a compilation of Ḥanafī–Shāfiʿī khilāfiyyāt on rahn (distinct from al-Kākī's al-Kifāyah fī Sharḥ al-Hidāyah). One example given: the rāhin is barred from reclaiming the marhūn temporarily for his own use [Ḥanafī] — since that defeats the required continuous detention — whereas per al-Shāfiʿī he's not barred, since occasional use doesn't conflict with the marhūn's designation for eventual sale.

New Matn — Dayn Maḍmūn Required

1.قَالَ: «وَلَا يَصِحُّ الرَّهْنُ إِلَّا بِدَيْنٍ مَضْمُونٍ» — rahn is valid only against a dayn maḍmūn. Sharḥ: ḥukmuhu = thubūt yad al-istīfāʾ, and istīfāʾ presupposes an established wujūb.
Ishkāl + jawāb — in-depth

The muḥaqqiq's own marginal gloss labels this exchange explicitly: "yadkhulu ʿalā hādhā al-lafẓ..." is glossed "ay yushkil" (i.e., this raises an ishkāl/difficulty), and "wa-yumkinu an yuqāla..." is glossed "fī'l-jawāb" (by way of response) — a named Ishkāl→Jawāb pair, not just a loose aside.

al-Ishkāl: this wording ("lā yaṣiḥḥu al-rahn illā bi-dayn maḍmūn") seems to exclude rahn taken against aʿyān maḍmūnah bi-anfusihā (e.g. a ghaṣb-claim on a specific object) — such rahn is valid even though there's technically no "dayn" yet.

al-Jawāb (per the majority of the mashāyikh): the wājib aṣlī in such cases is actually the qīmah (value) — returning the ʿayn itself is merely a mukhalliṣ (a way out/discharge), not the obligation itself — and that qīmah-obligation is a dayn. Proof: kafālah is valid for such claims too, and kafālah requires a dayn-type obligation. Even though the qīmah-obligation only becomes "due" after halāk, it becomes obligatory at the moment of halāk by virtue of the prior qabḍ — which is why its value is assessed as of [the day of that qabḍ — cuts off, full resolution on page 3 via al-Mabsūṭ's three-way classification].

Completing Page 2 — Dayn Maḍmūn (the ghaṣb-claim case)

1. Recap: page 2 ended mid-response to the objection that "lā yaṣiḥḥu al-rahn illā bi-dayn maḍmūn" seems to exclude pledging against a ghaṣb-claim (no "dayn" yet exists). Response: the wājib aṣlī there is the qīmah, which IS a dayn — returning the ʿayn is just a mukhalliṣ (way out), not the obligation itself.
al-Mabsūṭ's 3-way split of "rahn against an ʿayn"Ruling
Against an ʿayn that is amānah (no ḍamān attached at all)Bāṭil — rahn's mujib is yad al-istīfāʾ over the ʿayn; an amānah-holder's right is limited to that specific ʿayn, with no value-substitute path to recover from
Against an ʿayn maḍmūnah bi-ghayrihā (guaranteed via something ELSE) — e.g. a sold item still with the seller, guaranteed by the thamanAlso not allowed — it's already guaranteed via a substitute, not by itself
Against an ʿayn maḍmūnah bi-nafsihā (guaranteed by itself) — e.g. a ghaṣb claimValid — mūjib al-ʿaqd is: return the ʿayn if possible, else return its qīmah; that qīmah-obligation IS a dayn, recoverable from the marhūn's māliyyah
al-Kifāyah / al-Bināyah — al-Mabsūṭ citation (in-depth)

This three-way split is quoted from al-Sarakhsī's al-Mabsūṭ. The middle case (madmūnah bi-ghayrihā) is the one that most needs unpacking: a mabīʿ still sitting in the seller's hand before the buyer pays is already "guaranteed" — but via the thaman (the price owed), not via the ʿayn itself standing on its own as a dayn-generating claim. Since that guarantee-relationship is already occupied by something else, it can't ALSO serve as the basis for a NEW rahn. Only the third case — a pure ghaṣb-claim, where the ʿayn's own value directly becomes the fallback obligation — generates the kind of freestanding "dayn" that rahn requires. [al-Kifāyah 73/9] [al-Bināyah 559–560/11]

al-Kifāyah — worked example (in-depth)

al-Kākī's own numeric illustration: a thawb (garment) pledged, worth 10, against a dayn of 10. If its actual qīmah turns out to be only 5 — the murtahin has recourse against the rāhin for another 5 [to make up the shortfall]. If its qīmah is 15 — the excess of 5 is amānah per us [no extra liability on the murtahin]; per Zufar, the rāhin has recourse against the murtahin for that 5, since he holds rahn is maḍmūn bi'l-qīmah in full. [al-Kifāyah 75/9]

Ḥanafī rebuttal reasoning (al-Bināyah): the excess above the dayn is only pledged out of ḍarūrah (necessity) — you can't detain part of an object without detaining the whole — but there's no ḍarūrah requiring that excess to ALSO be ḍamān. ʿAlī's "yataraddān" report is reread as applying to the case of SALE (bayʿ) of the marhūn, not halāk — a different scenario entirely; another route from ʿAlī has him saying "al-murtahinu amīnun fī'l-faḍl" (the murtahin is a trustee regarding the excess), which directly supports the Ḥanafī reading. [al-Bināyah 561–562/11]

al-Dirāyah — the ʿAlī/ʿUmar/Ibn Masʿūd reports (in-depth)

ʿAlī "yataraddān al-faḍl": narrated by ʿAbd al-Razzāq (Muṣannaf no. 15039, Bāb al-Rahn Yahlik) and Ibn Abī Shaybah via al-Ḥakam ← ʿAlī. Also al-Bayhaqī via three DIFFERENT routes with varying wording — Khilās ← ʿAlī: "if there's excess in the rahn and it suffers a calamity (jāʾiḥah), then al-rahnu bi-mā fīh; if it does NOT suffer a calamity [i.e. sold normally], the excess IS returned"; al-Ḥārith ← ʿAlī: "if the rahn is worth more than the loan, or the loan more than the rahn, then it perishes — the excess is mutually returned"; Ibn al-Ḥanafiyyah ← ʿAlī: a further variant. Multiple routes, not fully uniform wording — worth knowing before treating it as one clean report.

ʿUmar: al-Bayhaqī narrates: "if it [the rahn] is less than what it secures, the full remainder of his right is returned to him; if more, he [murtahin] is amīn [regarding the excess]." al-Ṭaḥāwī narrates similarly (Sharḥ Maʿānī al-Āthār) via ʿUbayd ibn ʿUmayr ← ʿUmar. al-Bayhaqī himself notes this ʿUmar report "laysa bi-mashhūr" (not well-known) — though al-Bināyah adds "wa-laysa bi-jarḥ" (that's not the same as being flawed).

Ibn Masʿūd: Ibn Ḥajar: "lam arahu" — "I did not see it" [i.e. could not locate/verify al-Marghīnānī's attribution to Ibn Masʿūd specifically].

Methodological aside: Ibn Ḥajar also notes he could not find, as a discretely transmitted report, al-Marghīnānī's earlier framing (page 2) that "the Ṣaḥābah agreed rahn is maḍmūn and only differed on its kayfiyyah" — the individual reports exist, but that specific ijmāʿ-framing isn't something he could source as its own ḥadīth.

Masʾalah — Ḥuqūq al-Murtahin & Procedure of Collection

1. Murtahin may demand his dayn AND ḥabs (have the rāhin detained/imprisoned) for it — rahn only adds ṣiyānah, doesn't bar muṭālabah; ḥabs is the penalty once maṭl (stalling) is shown before the qāḍī.
2. When murtahin demands payment, rāhin is ordered to produce the marhūn FIRST, then dayn/receipt is delivered — mirrors sale mechanics (object shown, then price handed over). Avoids a double-istīfāʾ risk: collecting cash while still holding the pledge, then the pledge perishing, would be istīfāʾ twice.
3. Location rule: if the marhūn needs no ḥaml/muʾnah (carrying-cost, e.g. real estate) → rāhin must produce it regardless of distance. If it needs transport → murtahin just collects the dayn, rāhin not obligated to transport it (taslīm = takhliyah, not naql).

Selling via an Authorized ʿAdl (opens here)

1. If the rāhin authorized an ʿadl (impartial custodian) to sell the marhūn, a sale for cash OR credit is valid — the authorization was unrestricted. [Mechanics of what happens next continue on page 4.]

Ṣūrat al-Masʾalah

ʿAmr & Bakr

ʿAmr owes Bakr $1,000 and pledges a laptop appraised at exactly $1,000. It's destroyed in Bakr's hand (no fault of his) — since qīmah = dayn, Bakr is treated as fully paid; the $1,000 dayn is gone.

  • If the laptop was only worth $700: $700 falls off the dayn — Bakr still has recourse against ʿAmr personally for the remaining $300.
  • If the laptop was worth $1,300: Ḥanafī: the $300 excess is amānah — Bakr owes ʿAmr nothing extra. Zufar: ʿAmr can recover that $300 from Bakr.

ʿAdl-Sale Mechanics (completing page 3)

1. Sold via the ʿadl, price collected, murtahin demands dayn → murtahin must produce the price itself (badal qāʾim maqām al-mubdal — the substitute stands in for the substituted).
2. Contrast: pledged slave killed by mistake, qīmah owed by the killer's ʿāqilah over 3 years — rāhin is NOT compelled to pay until the ENTIRE qīmah is produced (qīmah = full khalaf/substitute for the whole ʿayn) — differs from the sale-price case because there the dayn arose by the rāhin's own action (authorizing the sale).
al-Kifāyah — the oath mechanism (in-depth)

An added nuance: when the rāhin disputes that the marhūn has perished (e.g. he's told it was sold but doubts the murtahin), the murtahin swears an oath that it has not perished, if the rāhin demands it — because the marhūn is ghāʾib (not physically present for immediate verification), so its halāk remains a live possibility (yaḥtamilu halākuhu). Once the murtahin takes the oath, the dayn must be paid. [al-Kifāyah 77/9] [al-ʿInāyah 77/9] [al-Bināyah 565/11]

al-Bināyah — "lammā qulnā" reasoning (in-depth)

The Ḥanafī reasoning behind the last row: once the sub-custodian juḥida (denies/disowns) the rahn and claims it as his own property, the property is deemed tawiya (irrecoverably lost) — and that loss falls on the murtahin, not the rāhin, because it was the murtahin's chain of custody (via his own ʿadl) that let it end up there. So istīfāʾ of the dayn is treated as having already been realized through that loss, and the murtahin no longer owns a claim to demand it again. This is the same "yad al-istīfāʾ" logic driving every ruling on this page — his hand is a recovery-oriented hand, so the risk of losing that hand's grip falls on him. [al-Kifāyah 78/9] [al-ʿInāyah 78/9]

Murtahin's Obligations & Limits

1. Can't force a sale until dayn is FULLY paid; may detain the WHOLE marhūn even after partial payment, until the remainder is recovered.
2. Once fully paid, must deliver the marhūn. If it perishes after full payment but before delivery → rāhin reclaims what he paid (halāk under the earlier qabḍ already counted as istīfāʾ — collecting the cash too would be istīfāʾ twice).
3. Mutual rescission (tafāsukh): murtahin may still detain until paid/released — rahn is voided only by ACTUAL return, not by the rescission itself. If it perishes after tafāsukh but before return, the dayn is still extinguished to the extent covered.
4. No benefit/use without the owner's permission (no riding, wearing, dwelling); can't sell/lease/lend it without the rāhin's authorization. Unauthorized use = taʿaddin (transgression) — doesn't void the rahn contract itself.
5. Safekeeping may be entrusted to himself, his wife, his child, or a household servant [continues page 5 — "like a wadīʿah"].

al-Bināyah — why house-rent is the one exception (in-depth)

Why does house-rent NOT follow the proportional-split rule that governs custodian's-wage and the runaway-slave reward? Because the obligation to pay a ju'l (the recovery-reward) or a wage arises specifically from ḍamān — it's compensation tied to what's guaranteed, so it's apportioned to match. House-rent arises from a different root entirely: the murtahin's ḥaqq al-ḥabs (right of detention), and that right applies to the WHOLE marhūn undivided — there's no "partial detention" of just the maḍmūn-share. Since the obligation follows the right, and the right is whole, the rent obligation is whole too, regardless of how much of the value happens to be amānah. [al-Bināyah 576/11] [al-Kifāyah 80/9]

1. Medical treatment, wound-care, and ransom-from-a-crime costs: split between the maḍmūn and amānah portions [completes page 6].

Medical/Ransom Costs (completing page 5)

1. Split proportionally between the maḍmūn and amānah portions of the marhūn's value — same logic as the ju'l rule on page 5.

Kharāj & ʿUshr

1. Kharāj (land tax) falls on the RĀHIN exclusively — it's a cost of ownership.
2. ʿUshr (tithe on produce) takes PRIORITY over the murtahin's right — it attaches to the ʿayn itself and doesn't void the rahn in the remainder. Unlike istiḥqāq (a successful third-party ownership claim), which WOULD void the whole rahn — because istiḥqāq negates ownership entirely, while ʿushr doesn't conflict with it.
al-Bināyah — al-Atrārī on the medical/ransom split (in-depth)

Al-Atrārī's explanation of the medical/ransom-cost split (page 5): the dayn that attaches to the rahn through istihlāk (destructive loss of a limb/part of it) concerns BOTH the rāhin and the murtahin — because a jināyah (injury) to the maḍmūn portion runs the same course as a jināyah against a ḍāmin party, so it falls on his [the murtahin's] own wealth; while a jināyah to the amānah portion runs like a jināyah against a wadīʿah, so it falls on the rāhin. Same value-proportional logic as every other cost-split ruling on this page. [al-Bināyah 577/11]

Kharāj vs. ʿushr, precisely: kharāj relates to the rāhin's ownership-right from the angle of māliyyah (its monetary burden), not the ʿayn itself — so it stays entirely his. ʿUshr, by contrast, attaches to the ʿayn (what the land produces) and is taken as a priority claim before anything reaches the murtahin — but only reduces the produce, doesn't touch the rahn's validity in what remains, unlike istiḥqāq which unravels the whole thing since a mushtaḥiqq (rightful third-party claimant)'s emergence means the marhūn was never validly the rāhin's to pledge at all. [al-ʿInāyah 81/9] [al-Bināyah 577/11]

al-Kifāyah — "mujarrad al-amr" nuance (in-depth)

Why does a mere qāḍī-order suffice for recourse, when a private request from the other party might not? Because a qāḍī-litigant can't practically escalate every small expense to court in person — so once he's ordered payment by the qāḍī (whether the other party was present or, per Abū Ḥanīfah's condition, absent and unreachable), it's binding as if the absent guardian/custodian himself had authorized it, since a qāḍī's wilāyah is general. But al-Dhakhīrah adds a caution: a mere bare command to spend, without the qāḍī actually FIXING the obligation onto the rāhin specifically, isn't enough by itself — the ruling has to actually bind the rāhin, not just record that spending was authorized in the abstract. [al-Kifāyah 82/9] [al-Bināyah 578/11]

Matn — Rahn al-Mushāʿ

1.لَا يَجُوزُ رَهْنُ الْمُشَاعِ — pledging a mushāʿ (undivided co-owned share) is invalid.
ḤanafīShāfiʿī
RulingInvalidValid
Angle 1 — ḥukmrahn's ḥukm = yad al-istīfāʾ, inconceivable over a mushāʿ sharehis ḥukm = designation for sale — a mushāʿ share CAN accept that
Angle 2 — dawāmrahn requires ḥabs dāʾim (perpetual detention); a mushāʿ share needs muhāyaʾah (turn-taking) → intermittent, like "I pledge you one day, not the next" — bars it regardless of divisibility(cf. hibah, which only bars indivisible items — a different rationale: cost of partition)
2. Pledging your share TO your own co-owner doesn't fix it either — same two-angle reasoning applies (he'd hold it one day by ownership, one day by rahn).
3. Subsequent shuyūʿ (a share becomes undivided AFTER a valid rahn, e.g. via inheritance): riwāyat al-aṣl voids the rahn's continuation too (analogized to maḥramiyyah in nikāḥ — a fundamental incapacity, not a fixable formality) — Abū Yūsuf: does NOT void continuation (continuation is easier than initiation, like hibah).
al-Bināyah / al-Kifāyah — full reasoning (in-depth)

Angle 1 in full: the impediment is ʿadam al-maḥalliyyah (the mushāʿ share simply isn't a valid subject for rahn's ḥukm) — so initiation and continuation are treated the same, exactly like maḥramiyyah in the chapter of nikāḥ. This differs from hibah, where a mushāʿ share DOES accept hibah's ḥukm (ownership) — the qabḍ requirement at hibah's initiation is only to negate gharāmat al-qismah (the burden objection), with no need to re-require it at the continuation stage. This is why rujūʿ (retraction) is valid in PART of a hibah, but faskh of a rahn contract is NOT valid in part.

Ṣūrat al-Masʾalah

ʿAmr & Bakr

ʿAmr and Bakr co-own a house 50/50. ʿAmr cannot pledge just his half-share to Bakr (or to anyone) as security for a debt — Bakr would only ever get yad al-istīfāʾ every other day via muhāyaʾah, breaking the required continuous detention.
