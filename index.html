Taʿrīf

1.الرَّهْنُ لُغَةً: حَبْسُ الشَّيْءِ — lughatan: detaining a thing.
2.شَرْعًا: جَعْلُ الشَّيْءِ مَحْبُوسًا بِحَقٍّ يُمْكِنُ اسْتِيفَاؤُهُ مِنْهُ كَالدَّيْنِ — sharʿan: a thing held as security for a ḥaqq (usually dayn), istīfāʾ obtainable from it.
al-Bināyah — in-depth

Lughah: al-ʿAynī notes the same root appears outside contracts: ﴿كُلُّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ رَهِينَةٌ﴾ [المدثر: ٣٨] — "every soul is rahīnah (held) for what it earned," i.e. detained by the consequence of its own misdeeds. Also: māʾun rāhin = still water, niʿmatun rāhinah = a lasting blessing — the root's core sense is fixity/permanence (al-thabāt wa'l-dawām). "Rahn" (the maṣdar) is also used to mean the marhūn itself; plural rahn/rihān.

Sharʿan — why "ḥaqq" not "dayn": deliberate, because rahn is valid not only for a dayn but also for a claim from ghaṣb (usurpation); ḥaqq covers both. "Kal-duyūn" is a deliberate exclusion (iḥtirāz) of pledging khamr (wine) and of taking a rahn to secure ḥudūd or qiṣāṣ — those don't admit istīfāʾ through a substitute value.

al-Qudduri's alternate taʿrīf: rahn shar'an is an ʿaqd wathīqah (contract of security) — distinguishing it from kafālah/ḥawālah (wathīqah bi-dhimmah, security attached to a person's liability) and from a sale-item still in the seller's hand.

Six-part breakdown al-ʿAynī gives: maʿnā lughatan · maʿnā sharʿan · sabab (the creditor's muṭālabah/demand for security) · rukn (ījāb alone per the Ḥanafīs; some require qabūl too) · ḥukm (milk ḥabs al-marhūn wa-ḥaqq al-muṭālabah bi'l-bayʿ) · ḥikmah (ḥuṣūl al-naẓar min al-jānibayn — mutual benefit to both sides).

Dalāʾil (mukhtaṣar)

1. Qurʾān — ﴿فَرِهَانٌ مَقْبُوضَةٌ﴾ [2:283]
2. Ḥadīth — النبي ﷺ اشترى طعامًا من يهودي ورهنه دِرْعَه (muttafaq ʿalayh)
3. Ijmāʿ — munʿaqid (undisputed)
4. Qiyās — wathīqah, like kafālah
Dalāʾil — in-depth

Qurʾān: the āyah was revealed about a debt made while traveling (safar) without a scribe available — Allāh permits substituting a rahn maqbūḍah for writing/witnessing. "Maqbūḍah" is itself the seed of the qabḍ discussion in Masʾalah 1.

Ḥadīth: the well-known report that the Prophet ﷺ bought food on credit from a Jewish merchant in Madīnah and left his armor as security — establishes both legitimacy and precedent (fiʿl) at once.

Ijmāʿ: beyond naql, there is no dispute among the fuqahāʾ that rahn as an institution is legitimate — the differences that follow (this whole kitāb) are about its details, not its basic mashrūʿiyyah.

Qiyās: rahn and kafālah are both wathāʾiq — kafālah secures fixing (wujūb) a debt onto a second person, rahn secures recovery (istīfāʾ) of a debt from a thing. Since kafālah is agreed legitimate, so is rahn.

Masʾalah 1 — Inʿiqād vs. Tamām

Matnالرَّهْنُ يَنْعَقِدُ بِالْإِيجَابِ وَالْقَبُولِ وَيَتِمُّ بِالْقَبْضِ
RuknḤanafī ījāb alone (ʿaqd tabarruʿ, like hibah); qabḍ = sharṭ al-luzūmMālik yalzam bi-nafs al-ʿaqd (māl both sides → like bayʿ)
Qabḍ (manqūl)ẓāhir riwāyah: takhliyah kāfī (asaḥḥ)Abū Yūsuf naql required (ḍamān yabtadiʾ, mithl ghaṣb)
Masʾalah 1 — in-depth

Ḥanafī rukn: rukn is ījāb by itself, since rahn is ʿaqd tabarruʿ — it is completed by the mutabarriʿ alone (like hibah/ṣadaqah); qabḍ is only sharṭ al-luzūm, not a rukn. Rukn = without which the ʿaqd doesn't exist; sharṭ al-luzūm = needed only for bindingness.

Mālik's view & why: rahn is lāzim the instant ījāb+qabūl occur — (a) unlike a pure gift it has māl on both sides (marhūn vs. dayn), closer to bayʿ; (b) it's a wathīqah like kafālah, which binds on ʿaqd alone.

Ḥanafī rebuttal: (1) grammar — "فَرِهَانٌ مَّقْبُوضَةٌ": a maṣdar-phrase right after fāʾ in a conditional's jawāb functions as a command in Arabic usage — Allāh in effect commands "let there be a pledge, taken into possession," placing qabḍ at the heart of what makes rahn function. (2) rahn is tabarruʿ — the rāhin gets nothing owed in return and cannot be judicially compelled (yujbaru), so it needs a concrete imḍāʾ (execution) to finalize, as in waṣiyyah — that imḍāʾ is qabḍ.

Takhliyah vs. naql: ẓāhir al-riwāyah — mere takhliyah (clearing access, e.g. handing over keys) suffices, by analogy to qabḍ of the mabīʿ in a sale. Abū Yūsuf differs for manqūlāt specifically: this qabḍ originates ḍamān from scratch (ibtidāʾan), like ghaṣb — unlike a buyer's qabḍ, which merely shifts existing ḍamān from seller to buyer — so he requires actual physical naql. Al-Marghīnānī: the first view (takhliyah) is asaḥḥ.

Masʾalah 2 — Sharṭ al-Qabḍ & Khiyār

1.قَبَضَهُ مَحُوزًا مُفَرَّغًا مُتَمَيِّزًا → تَمَّ الْعَقْدُ → lazima
2. Before qabḍ → rāhin bi'l-khiyār: yusallim aw yarjiʿ ʿan al-rahn (maqṣūd not yet ḥāṣil)
Masʾalah 2 — in-depth

The marhūn must be maḥūz (fully gathered into the murtahin's hold), mufarragh (cleared of any competing use/mixture), and mutamayyiz (clearly identifiable as this specific object, not a vague undivided share) — only then is qabḍ "complete" and the ʿaqd reaches tamām/luzūm.

Before that: the rāhin keeps full khiyār — he isn't yet bound and may simply retract, because the maqṣūd of rahn (securing the murtahin's ability to obtain istīfāʾ) only functions once he actually holds the marhūn — so luzūm cannot attach any earlier than qabḍ.

Masʾalah 3 — Ḍamān al-Marhūn

ḤanafīShāfiʿī
Statusmaḍmūn — دخل في ضمانهamānah
If halākdayn reduced by qīmah of marhūndayn stays in full — rāhin loses marhūn for nothing
Dalīlﷺ to murtahin (horse died): «ذَهَبَ حَقُّكَ»«لَا يَغْلَقُ الرَّهْنُ… لِصَاحِبِهِ غُنْمُهُ وَعَلَيْهِ غُرْمُهُ» + qiyās ʿalā halāk al-ṣakk
Masʾalah 3 — in-depth

Shāfiʿī's case: the marhūn is pure amānah — the murtahin bears no ḍamān. If it's destroyed without his fault, the rāhin still owes the full dayn. Dalīl: "lā yaghlaq al-rahn... lahu ghunmuhu wa-ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" — any ghunm (growth) of the marhūn belongs to the rāhin, so any ghurm (loss) is his too. Qiyās: rahn is like a debt-document (ṣakk) — losing the paper doesn't erase the debt; also, the whole point of a rahn is to increase ṣiyānah (security) for the creditor — if the debt could vanish whenever the marhūn is destroyed, that defeats the purpose of taking security at all.

Marghīnānī's gloss on the same ḥadīth: "lā yaghlaq" means the marhūn does not automatically become the murtahin's property in full settlement merely upon the rāhin's default (no automatic foreclosure) — not that it carries zero ḍamān. Same naṣṣ, opposite reading.

Ḥanafī rebuttal (opens here, continues page 2): ﷺ told a murtahin whose pledged horse died, "dhahaba ḥaqquka" — your right (to that extent) is gone — directly supporting that halāk of the marhūn does extinguish part of the dayn, because the murtahin is ḍāmin, not a mere amānah-holder.

Takhrīj — al-Dirāyah (quick grading)

1000 (buying food, pledged armor) — muttafaq ʿalayh, ʿĀʾishah. Wordings vary: ilā ajal / dirʿ min ḥadīd / thalāthīn ṣāʿ.
1001 (lā yaghlaq al-rahn) — al-Dāraquṭnī: ḥasan muttaṣil; most routes mursal (al-Zuhrī chains). Tanbīh: "qālahā thalāthan" — Ibn Ḥajar: lam ajidhu (unverified).
1002 (dhahaba ḥaqquka) — mursal (Abū Dāwūd al-Marāsīl, via ʿAṭāʾ; also Ibn Abī Shaybah).
al-Dirāyah — full isnād notes

1001 — chains in detail: (a) Ibn Ḥibbān, via Sufyān ← Ziyād ibn Saʿd ← al-Zuhrī ← Saʿīd ← Abū Hurayrah; ṣaḥḥaḥahu al-Ḥākim. (b) al-Dāraquṭnī, muttaṣil route, graded ḥasan muttaṣil; also authenticated by ʿAbd al-Ḥaqq, accepted by Ibn ʿAbd al-Barr. (c) ʿAbd al-Razzāq ← Maʿmar ← al-Zuhrī ← Ibn al-Musayyab — mursal; includes al-Zuhrī confirming this addresses the pre-Islamic saying "if I don't bring your money, the rahn is yours," with the addition "if it perishes, this one's right does not disappear — only the value perishes from the rahn's owner; lahu ghunmuhu wa-ʿalayhi ghurmuhu." (d) Same mursal chain also via al-Thawrī, Ibn Abī Shaybah/Wakīʿ, and al-Shāfiʿī/Ibn Abī Fudayk — all ← Ibn Abī Dhiʾb ← al-Zuhrī. Al-Shāfiʿī's own gloss: ghunmuhu = increase, ghurmuhu = decrease and halāk. (e) Abū Dāwūd (al-Marāsīl): notes "lahu ghunmuhu wa-ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" may be Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab's own words (mudraj), not the Prophet's ﷺ. Parallel report from Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī describes the jāhilī practice the ḥadīth corrects.

Tanbīh: Ibn Ḥajar explicitly flags "qālahā thalāthan" (said it three times) as "lam ajidhu" — not found in any source he traced. Worth knowing before citing that detail as established.

1000: Ibn Ḥajar cross-refers that something on this ḥadīth already preceded at the start of Kitāb al-Buyūʿ.

1002: via ʿAṭāʾ — a man pledged a horse, it died in the murtahin's hand, Prophet ﷺ said "dhahaba ḥaqquka" — mursal in both Abū Dāwūd's Marāsīl and Ibn Abī Shaybah. The entire Ḥanafī rebuttal on this page rests on a mursal report — al-Marghīnānī will need to argue for its acceptability, or argue the ruling by other means, on the next page.

Ṣūrat al-Masʾalah

ʿAmr & Bakr

ʿAmr owes Bakr $1,000 dayn. Pledges a laptop (qīmah $800) — ījāb; Bakr qabūl.

Before qabḍ: ʿAmr may still rajaʿa (khiyār) — Bakr can't force delivery.

After qabḍ, halāk (no fault of Bakr):

  • Ḥanafī: $800 of dayn extinguished → ʿAmr owes $200.
  • Shāfiʿī: ʿAmr still owes the full $1,000.
Walkthrough — in-depth

Scenario A (before qabḍ): ʿAmr offers the laptop (ījāb), Bakr accepts (qabūl) — the ʿaqd is munʿaqid but not yet lāzim (it's tabarruʿ, like hibah). Before ʿAmr actually hands it over, he changes his mind — since qabḍ hasn't happened, this is within his khiyār and Bakr cannot compel delivery. Had ʿAmr delivered it and Bakr received it maḥūzan mufarraghan mutamayyizan, the ʿaqd becomes lāzim and ʿAmr can no longer withdraw.

Scenario B (after qabḍ, halāk occurs): ʿAmr delivers, Bakr takes proper qabḍ — the laptop is now marhūn in Bakr's ḍamān. A week later, with no fault of Bakr's, it's destroyed (say, stolen beyond recovery).

Per al-Shāfiʿī: the laptop was amānah in Bakr's hand — Bakr bears no risk, so none of ʿAmr's dayn is reduced; ʿAmr still owes the full $1,000 and has lost his $800 laptop for nothing.

Per the Ḥanafī view (the position al-Marghīnānī is building toward, via "dhahaba ḥaqquka"): the laptop was maḍmūn in Bakr's possession, not mere amānah — its halāk operates as an implicit istīfāʾ up to its qīmah. $800 of the dayn is treated as satisfied, so ʿAmr now owes Bakr only the remaining $200.

Masʾalah 3 (cont.) — Ḥanafī Rebuttal

1.إِذَا غُمِّيَ الرَّهْنُ فَهُوَ بِمَا فِيهِ — if the marhūn's value becomes unclear after halāk, it's valued by estimation.
2. Ijmāʿ of Ṣaḥābah & Tābiʿīn: marhūn is maḍmūn (though they differ on kayfiyyah) — al-Marghīnānī: "the amānah view breaks this ijmāʿ" (khariq lahu).
3.لَا يَغْلَقُ الرَّهْنُ reread: = no automatic total forfeiture/ownership-transfer to the murtahin — not "no ḍamān" (per al-Karkhī, from the Salaf).
al-Kifāyah — kayfiyyat al-ḍamān (in-depth)

Point 1's "ishtabahat" (if the value becomes uncertain) is glossed: if the rāhin says "I don't know how much it was worth" and the murtahin says the same, it's valued as estimated (this taʾwīl is narrated from Abū Ja'far).

The Ṣaḥābah's kayfiyyah dispute (why al-Marghīnānī says "mukhtalifūn fī kayfiyyatihi" but still ijmāʿ on the core): Abū Bakr & ʿAlī — maḍmūn bi-qīmatihi (by its full value); ʿUmar & Ibn Masʿūd — maḍmūn bi'l-aqall min qīmatihi wa min al-dayn (by whichever is less, its value or the debt); Ibn ʿAbbās — maḍmūn bi'l-dayn (by the debt amount itself). All agree ḍamān exists — they only differ on the measure. [al-Kifāyah 70/9]

"lā yaghlaq al-rahn" — full gloss: no one from the language/fiqh scholars understood this as negating ḍamān from the murtahin. al-Karkhī reports from the Salaf (including Ṭāwūs, Ibrāhīm al-Nakhaʿī) that they agreed the meaning is: the marhūn does not become owned by the murtahin merely by the dayn's term expiring — refuting the jāhilī practice (via al-Zuhrī) where people stipulated "if I don't pay by such a date, the rahn is yours." The Prophet ﷺ nullified that stipulation with "lā yaghlaq al-rahn." A minority (Saʿīd ibn al-Musayyab) read it as "the rāhin isn't bound by a term-based sale unless he actually sells at that time" — same conclusion, different route.

"lahu ghunmuhu wa ʿalayhi ghurmuhu" reread by the Ḥanafīs: ghunm = zawāʾid (increase/offspring of the marhūn) belongs to the rāhin (like a muḍārib analogy — the "owner of capital" gets the growth); ghurm = the rāhin bears the marhūn's own decrease in value, and if halāk occurs, "the meaning of ghurm" is specifically: nafaqat al-rahn ḥāl qiyāmihi, wa'l-kafan ḥāl mawtihi (its upkeep while alive, its shroud if it dies) — i.e. these two specific costs fall on the rāhin, not that the whole dayn survives halāk. [al-Kifāyah 71/9]

Dalīl — Yad al-Istīfāʾ & Ḥikmah

1. Thābit li'l-murtahin: yad al-istīfāʾ = milk al-yad wa'l-ḥabs (possessory right aimed at eventual recovery) — because rahn implies continuous detention (ḥabs dāʾim).
2. Qurʾān parallel — ﴿كُلُّ نَفْسٍ بِمَا كَسَبَتْ رَهِينَةٌ﴾ [74:38]; poetic shāhid (bayt of Zuhayr): وَفَارَقْتُكَ بِرَهْنٍ لَا فِكَاكَ لَهُ … يَوْمَ الْوَدَاعِ فَأَمْسَى الرَّهْنُ قَدْ غَلِقَا
3. Ḥikmah: rahn = wathīqah for istīfāʾ → gives amn min al-juḥūd (security against denial) + makes rāhin ʿājiz ʿan al-intifāʿ → rāhin hastens to pay the dayn.

Core Ḥanafī Logic — the Ribā Point

StepPoint
1Istīfāʾ thābit min wajh, taqarrara bi'l-halāk — halāk of the marhūn is a (partial) satisfaction of the dayn, now confirmed/settled.
2Law istawfāhu thāniyan → yuʾaddī ilā'l-ribā — if the murtahin then collected the full dayn too, that's a double recovery = ribā.
3Bi-khilāf ḥālat al-qiyām — while the marhūn is intact, this "notional" istīfāʾ is undone by returning it, so no double-dip issue there.
4Istīfāʾ occurs via al-māliyyah (the value); the ʿayn itself remains amānah — hence nafaqah/kafan of the marhūn fall on the rāhin, not the murtahin.
Also: qabḍ al-rahn does NOT substitute for qabḍ al-sharāʾ if the murtahin later buys the marhūn — amānah-qabḍ can't stand in for ḍamān-qabḍ.

Rebutting Shāfiʿī's Ṣiyānah Argument

1. Muqtaḍā al-ʿaqd = thubūt yad al-istīfāʾ — this is what realizes ṣiyānah (not despite halāk-istīfāʾ, but because of it).
2. Barāʾat al-dhimmah (discharge) is min ḍarūrātihi (a necessary side-effect), not the primary maqṣūd — same structure as ḥawālah.
Uṣūl summary + branch masʾalah — in-depth

al-Ḥāṣil (the upshot): for us [Ḥanafīs], ḥukm al-rahn = the marhūn becomes muḥtabas (held) against its dayn by establishing yad al-istīfāʾ over it. For him [al-Shāfiʿī], the dayn attaches to the ʿayn such that istīfāʾ only ever happens via an actual sale (bayʿ) — no automatic value-transfer on halāk.

Al-Marghīnānī notes several masāʾil branch from these two uṣūl, which he says he enumerated in a work called Kifāyat al-Muntahā — a compilation of Ḥanafī–Shāfiʿī khilāfiyyāt on rahn (distinct from al-Kākī's al-Kifāyah fī Sharḥ al-Hidāyah). One example given: the rāhin is barred from reclaiming the marhūn temporarily for his own use [Ḥanafī] — since that defeats the required continuous detention — whereas per al-Shāfiʿī he's not barred, since occasional use doesn't conflict with the marhūn's designation for eventual sale.

New Matn — Dayn Maḍmūn Required

1.قَالَ: «وَلَا يَصِحُّ الرَّهْنُ إِلَّا بِدَيْنٍ مَضْمُونٍ» — rahn is valid only against a dayn maḍmūn. Sharḥ: ḥukmuhu = thubūt yad al-istīfāʾ, and istīfāʾ presupposes an established wujūb.
Objection + response — in-depth

Objection: this wording seems to exclude rahn taken against aʿyān maḍmūnah bi-anfusihā (e.g. a ghaṣb-claim on a specific object) — such rahn is valid even though there's technically no "dayn" yet.

Response (per the majority of the mashāyikh): the wājib aṣlī in such cases is actually the qīmah (value) — returning the ʿayn itself is merely a mukhalliṣ (a way out/discharge), not the obligation itself — and that qīmah-obligation is a dayn. Proof: kafālah is valid for such claims too, and kafālah requires a dayn-type obligation. Even though the qīmah-obligation only becomes "due" after halāk, it becomes obligatory at the moment of halāk by virtue of the prior qabḍ — which is why its value is assessed as of [the day of that qabḍ — cuts off, continues page 3].
